This Privacy Policy also explains the choices you have in relation to these processing activities.

1.2 This Privacy Policy does not apply to non-iProERP websites, services, products, applications or social networks (“non-iProERP offerings”) that you may link to or connect with when interacting on iProERP websites. Use of non-iProERP offerings may lead to parties other than iProERP processing your personal information. iProERP does not have any control over non-iProERP offerings and you should review the privacy policies of these parties before using the non-iProERP offerings.

1.3 In this Privacy Policy:

1.3.1 “you” is a reference to any person, including a natural person, a company, a body corporate, an association, a joint venture, a partnership, a trust and any entity capable of suing and being sued, who visit and/or use iProERP websites or social media pages, attendees at iProERP events, customers and prospective customers and their representatives, suppliers and channel partners and their representatives and iProERP’s employees;

1.3.2 “personal information” means information that relates to an identified or identifiable person such as a name, identification number, location data or online identifier;

1.3.3 “process” means any operation or set of operations that is performed on personal information, including the use of, recording, copying, compiling, collecting, collating, structuring, processing, mining, storing, erasing, altering, disclosing by transmission, disseminating and/or transferring such personal information;

1.3.4 “iProERP offerings” means any one or more of iProERP’s products, services, PartnerUP Program, social media pages, non-marketing related newsletters, case studies, blogs, education services and materials, webinars, events and iProERP websites and the services, tools, content and features made available through the iProERP websites; and

1.3.5 the interpretation of general words shall not be restricted by being followed by words including a particular class of acts, matters or things or by being followed by particular examples.

3. WHAT INFORMATION iProERP COLLECTS ABOUT YOU

3.1 iProERP may collect information directly from you, including:

3.1.1 your name, physical address, email address, telephone numbers and/or your identification number and, in the case of a natural person, your company’s name, your company’s identification number, your job title, your business telephone number and/or email address, such as when you submit an enquiry to ProERP, when you contract with iProERP for a iProERP offering or when you create an online account or profile to access iProERP offerings;

3.1.2 your payment information if you purchase or subscribe for iProERP offerings that are provided by way of iProERP websites or if iProERP needs to process a payment to you. However in most instances when you pay ProERP for a iProERP offering by way of a iProERP website, your payment information will be transferred directly from you to the third party service provider that processes the payment without iProERP having access to your payment information;

3.1.3 your financial information for vetting purposes if you apply to become a channel partner and, if you are a channel partner, for purposes of assessing your performance and determining your grading and benefits;

3.1.4 your company logo for display on iProERP websites as recognition of the relationship that exists between you and ProERP, whether as a customer, a channel partner or a supplier;

3.1.5 the passwords and log-in details that you have created to access your account with iProERP in respect of the iProERP offerings; and/or

3.1.6 your opinions relating to iProERP offerings received from surveys.

3.2 iProERP may also collect other information through your interaction with and use of iProERP offerings, including:

3.2.1 Internet Protocol address (“IP address”) and information derived from your IP address, such as your geographic location, your company or when you access iProERP websites;

3.2.2 information about the device that you use to access iProERP websites, such as device type and version, mobile device identifier, browser type, operating system, the presence or use of applications or information about your preferred language;

3.2.3 information about the company that you work for, such as the name, size, location, industry or its public officers;

3.2.4 behavioural or demographic attributes, when tied to personal information that identifies you;

3.2.5 behavioural data about the internet connected device you use when interacting with iProERP websites, such as webpages clicked, content areas visited and time spent on webpages, content downloaded, date and time of activities, how often you access iProERP websites or the web search used to locate and navigate to a iProERP website;

3.2.6 transactional data, including products and services that you have enquired about or ordered or are using;

3.2.7 your activities in relation to iProERP offerings, such as the applications you have purchased through the iProERP Appstore, the support issues you have logged, the certification you have achieved or, in the case of a channel partner, the customers that you have sublicensed iProERP products to;

3.2.8 your use and navigation of iProERP websites and your use of the services, products, tools and material made available by way of iProERP websites; and/or

3.2.9 aggregated information relating to the use of iProERP offerings, such as which features or aspects of iProERP offerings are used the most or the least or any errors or problems that are registered in respect of specific features or aspects of the iProERP offerings.

3.3 In some instances, iProERP may combine other information with personal information. If iProERP combines other information with personal information, iProERP will treat the combined information as personal information.

4. HOW iProERP COLLECTS INFORMATION

4.1 ProERP, its affiliates and its third party service providers may collect information from a variety of sources including:

4.1.1 when you provide the information to iProERP or its third party service providers either directly or through iProERP websites;

4.1.2 your interaction with ProERP, your use of iProERP offerings and your access to and activities on or in relation to iProERP offerings;

4.1.3 the use of technologies on iProERP websites such as:

4.1.3.1 electronic communication protocols – when you visit iProERP websites, iProERP may automatically receive information from you as part of the communication connection itself, which may include the information listed in clauses 3.2.2 or 3.2.5 above, and iProERP may also automatically receive and record information about your interaction with iProERP websites;

4.1.3.2 cookies – are small text files sent by a web server to store information on your browser so it can later be read back from that browser. Cookies collect information such as your device ID, your IP address, behavioural data of your device usage on iProERP websites and general usage and volume statistical information. Cookies remember log-in details and provide secure log-in, keep track of information you have provided to ProERP, remember your task or transaction progress, analyse site usage to provide custom content, conduct analytics to optimise iProERP website functionality, ensure the iProERP websites look consistent and allow you to post comments. You can set your browser to alert you when a cookie is being used and accept, reject or disable the cookie. If you reject or disable a cookie, you may not be able to use certain functions or features of a iProERP website;

4.1.3.3 tags (often called pixels) – are small blocks of code on a web page that allow iProERP websites to do things like read and place cookies on your browser or device. iProERP uses tags to collect information about you when you visit iProERP websites, such as your IP address, the time that you visited the iProERP website and the type of browser you used, and your behaviour on the iProERP websites, such as which webpages you accessed or what information you downloaded; and/or

4.1.3.4 analytics tools – these tools may establish your location, track how you use iProERP websites or create a profile of you. This assists iProERP to determine which content is the most appropriate to be displayed while you visit iProERP websites, which iProERP offerings you are interested in and how you are using the information that is made available to you on iProERP websites; and/or

4.1.4 other data you may have made publicly available, such as social media posts or when you ‘like’ ProERP, share content or follow iProERP on Twitter or data provided by third party sources, such as professional networking sites or information service providers.
